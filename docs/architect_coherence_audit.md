# Architect — System Coherence Audit (DPSim v0.1.0)

**System verdict: COHERENT_WITH_GAPS.**

## Executive Summary

The clean-slate architecture (`Quantity → ResolvedParameter → ProcessStep → ProcessRecipe → ResultGraph → ProcessDossier`, per `docs/03_architecture_modification_plan.md` §2.3) is correctly designed and honestly implemented at the **boundary** — `recipe_resolver.resolve_lifecycle_inputs` produces a typed `LifecycleResolvedInputs` with full unit and provenance plumbing, and the lifecycle orchestrator wires `ResultGraph` nodes for stage-level transitions. But the architecture is **not consumed end-to-end**: at the boundary into M2 and M3 solvers, `Quantity` is unwrapped to bare `float`, and from that point inward the legacy `SimulationParameters / FullResult / FunctionalMicrosphere / FunctionalMediaContract` contracts are load-bearing. The `dev-orchestrator` plan for v0.2.0 + v0.3.0 closes the most visible scope-of-claim and UI gaps but does **not** retire the legacy data contracts, propagate `Quantity` into solver internals, lift `ResultGraph` registration into individual solvers, or generalise the `ModelMode` (`empirical_engineering` / `hybrid_coupled` / `mechanistic_research`) branch beyond a single L4 modulus path. Coherence is recoverable in v0.4.0 with a focused "internalise-the-clean-slate" milestone; without it, every future M2/M3 enhancement has two homes (recipe + legacy) and the evidence-tier chain has silent drops.

## Cross-Cutting Pattern

The eight HIGH and MEDIUM findings collapse to **one pattern**: the clean-slate primitives (`Quantity`, `ResolvedParameter`, `ResultGraph`, `ModelManifest`, `ModelMode`, `ModelEvidenceTier` enum) are **boundary types** in the current implementation. They appear at recipe ingestion, at lifecycle output, in dossier export, and in the validation report — but the load-bearing inside-the-pipeline contracts remain `SimulationParameters` (legacy dataclass with bare floats), `FullResult` (legacy), `FunctionalMicrosphere` (legacy + mutable, with string `confidence_tier`), and bare-float function signatures inside M2 and M3. The dev-orchestrator's v0.2.0 + v0.3.0 plan does excellent work at the **edges** (gradient elution evidence chain, family × reagent matrix, UI badges, recipe-only M1 tab) but does not push the typed primitives **inward**. After v0.3.0, two parallel architectures will still coexist.

## Top-3 System-Level Deficits That v0.3.0 Does Not Close

### Deficit 1 — Internal `Quantity` plumbing in M2/M3 solvers
**What:** Solver function signatures, internal state, and intermediate dataclasses (`BreakthroughResult`, `ChromatographyMethodResult`, `LoadedStateElutionResult`, `M1WashingResult`, `CrosslinkingResult`, etc.) all use bare floats with implicit units. Unit drift bugs are silently possible.
**Why it matters:** v0.4 calibration ingest expansion (P5++ MC LRM, additional isotherm shape posteriors) requires that every parameter touched by an MC sample has machine-readable units. Bare floats make that retrofit O(touched-parameters); typed `Quantity` makes it O(parameters-in-MC).
**Scope:** ~40 dataclass fields across 8 result types; ~25 solver function signatures. ~600 LOC mechanical conversion + 1 architect-tier protocol. Single milestone.

### Deficit 2 — `ModelMode` enforcement in M2/M3
**What:** `EMPIRICAL_ENGINEERING / HYBRID_COUPLED / MECHANISTIC_RESEARCH` is defined and consumed only in L4. M2 and M3 ignore the field entirely; users get identical outputs across modes.
**Why it matters:** The architecture's central promise that "the simulator should not claim universal quantitative prediction until local calibration exists" is enforced only on M1's bead modulus. The Protein A isotherm should refuse to produce a numeric DBC in `empirical_engineering` mode without a calibrated FMC; today it does. Deficit-1 (Quantity) and Deficit-2 (ModelMode) jointly enable mode-aware output downgrade.
**Scope:** Mode-conditional gating in 6–8 M2/M3 entry points + manifest-level mode recording. ~250 LOC. Single milestone.

### Deficit 3 — Bin-resolved DSD propagation
**What:** Per `lifecycle/orchestrator.py:1128`, the lifecycle samples `(0.10, 0.50, 0.90)` quantiles. The full DSD payload from M1 (`BeadSizeDistributionPayload` with `n_bins` typically 10–30) is **available** but discarded after representative selection. v0.2.0 module **A4** opens the door (`run_full_method=True` per quantile) but keeps the 3-quantile contract.
**Why it matters:** d10/d90 representatives miss the long tail that drives wall channeling and bed compression. A 30-bin breakthrough integration over a real DSD changes DBC predictions by 10–30 % vs the d50 representative on dispersed feeds. The scientific-advisor's M1-S1 finding (overclaim from single-representative collapse) cannot be retired without it.
**Scope:** Refactor `_dsd_representative_rows` into `_dsd_bin_iterator` with mass-weighted aggregation; introduce `joblib`-parallel per-bin method runs (the A4 API is already shaped for this). ~400 LOC + scientific-advisor brief on bin-weighting scheme. 1.5 milestones.

---

These three deficits — internal Quantity, ModelMode enforcement, bin-resolved DSD — are the v0.4.0 architectural milestone. After that milestone the legacy `SimulationParameters` / `FullResult` path can be deprecated and removed in v0.5.0. Until then, the system is **COHERENT_WITH_GAPS**: usable, scientifically honest at its weakest tier, but carrying two parallel architectures that will diverge unless retired on a schedule.
